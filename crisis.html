Crisis Support

What is a Mental Health Crisis?

A mental health crisis is when someone feels unable to cope with their feelings or situation. This might include thoughts of harming yourself, severe anxiety, or feeling completely overwhelmed.

What to Do in a Crisis

If you are in crisis:

  1. Tell someone immediately
  2. Call a helpline (Samaritans 116 123 or Childline 0800 1111)
  3. Go to your local A&E department
  4. Call your GP for emergency appointment
  5. Call 999 if you are in immediate danger

If someone else is in crisis:

  1. Stay calm and listen
  2. Take them seriously
  3. Call for help (999, helpline, or GP)
  4. Stay with them if possible
  5. Be supportive

If You Are Thinking About Harming Yourself

Please know that:

  • These feelings can pass with time and support
  • Help is available
  • People care about you
  • Your life has value

Please reach out:

  • Call Samaritans: 116 123
  • Call Childline: 0800 1111
  • Text HELLO to 50808
  • Call 999
  • Tell a trusted adult

After a Crisis

After a crisis, follow up with professional support. Develop a safety plan and identify your warning signs.
