Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between feeling sad and having depression?

It is normal to feel sad sometimes. Depression is a mental health condition that lasts longer and affects how you function in daily life. If sadness lasts for weeks and affects your sleep, appetite, or ability to do things you enjoy, it might be depression. If you are concerned, speak to your GP.

Is anxiety normal?

Yes, everyone feels anxious sometimes. This is normal. However, if anxiety is persistent, intense, or prevents you from doing everyday activities, it might be helpful to speak to someone about it.

Can mental health problems be cured?

Many mental health conditions can be treated and managed effectively. With the right support, people can feel much better and regain control of their lives.

Is it weak to ask for help?

No, absolutely not. Asking for help shows strength and self-awareness. Everyone needs support sometimes.

What should I do if my friend is struggling?

Listen to them without judgment, take their feelings seriously, and encourage them to seek professional help. Be there for them and let them know you care.

Are mental health services confidential?

Yes, mental health services are confidential. However, there are some exceptions if you are at immediate risk of harming yourself or others.

What if I cannot afford mental health support?

Mental health support is available for free on the NHS in the UK. You can access it through your GP or local mental health services.

When should I see a doctor about mental health?

See your GP if you have been feeling low, anxious, or unwell for more than two weeks, or if your feelings are affecting your daily life.

Is it possible to recover from mental health problems?

Yes, many people recover fully from mental health problems. With the right support and treatment, you can feel better and get back to enjoying life.
